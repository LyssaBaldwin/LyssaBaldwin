Who is Gordon Moore?


      Image 1

Gordon E. Moore is an American Engineer and Entrepreneur, most known for being the co-founder of Intel Corporation. He is known for being the ‘Silicon Valley’ Pioneer, and thanks to him light years of technological innovation are now at our forefront! It's

Growing up, Moore was introduced to chemistry at a young age and would spend hours working on experiments and happily engaged in making explosives. He pursued his bachelors in Chemistry at the University of California, Berkeley. He then earned a doctorate in physical chemistry at the California Institute of Technology in 1954. 

After pursuing his PhD. he was disappointed with the nature of his research job at John Hopkins University and decided to come back west to work for a private company, Shockley Semiconductor. However, less than a year after joining him and a group of engineers and scientists rebelled against the company’s CEO and his heavy-handed management and founded Fairchild Semiconductor.


Moore quickly rose from engineer manager to becoming the company’s executive director. Among his contributions the first integrated circuit, commonly known as “the chip” - was created. This chip is a thin slice of silicon that has been specially processed so that a tiny electric circuit is chemically etched on its surface. The circuit can contain many millions of transistors that connect electrically to perform a function. Thanks to this technology all computers use chips nowadays. Through the efforts of Moore and others, Intel is the world’s largest chip maker and his company is valued at a net worth of $240 billion dollars, making it one of the world’s most powerful and successful companies. 


Among many honors Moore received the National Medal of Technology and the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the United States’ highest civilian honor. The Society of Chemical Industry awarded him the Perkin Medal and named an annual award after his name, which recognizes early-career success in innovation.
